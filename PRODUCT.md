# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bank relationship managers and corporate clients completing a corporate bank account opening application via Straight2Bank (S2B). They are in a task — completing a multi-section onboarding workflow — and need clarity, confidence, and progress visibility at every step. The primary demo audience is senior management evaluating the onboarding UX.

## Product Purpose

S2BO Module 1 V2 is an interactive prototype of the Straight2Bank corporate onboarding flow. It demonstrates two UX paradigms (Spec A: structured form entry with registry assist; Spec B: AI-embedded document intelligence) across two client scenarios (Meridian: established company; Aurelius: newly incorporated SPV). Success means the demo lands as credible, production-grade banking software.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, precise, trusted. Standard Chartered brand. The interface should feel like enterprise banking — not a startup app, not a government form.

## Anti-references

Not specified by the user. Avoid accidental consumer fintech playfulness (bright pastels, bouncy motion, oversized rounded corners that read as mobile-consumer). Avoid bureaucratic form heaviness. The interface should feel like Standard Chartered's own digital banking products.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity in complexity** — multi-section workflows must always show the user where they are and what comes next; never leave them lost in a form.
2. **Consistent affordances** — every button, input, and control must look and behave the same way across all sections; a single inconsistency undermines credibility.
3. **Authority without heaviness** — the visual weight should feel confident and institutional, not slow or bureaucratic; white space and restraint carry more weight than decoration.
4. **Earned motion** — transitions mark state changes and section transitions, not decoration; users are in a task.
5. **Demo-ready** — every visible state must be plausible to a senior executive; no placeholder text, no half-finished states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Interactive controls must have visible focus states. No accessibility requirements specified beyond standard.
